Why It Matters

Dirty Panels Are Costing You Money Every Day

Solar panels are a smart investment — but without proper maintenance, their performance can significantly decline. Properties across the Mornington Peninsula are constantly exposed to:

  • Salt spray from sea air
  • Fine dust and airborne debris
  • Bird droppings and tree sap
  • Pollen and organic build-up
  • Pollution and coastal grime

Over time, this debris blocks sunlight reaching the photovoltaic cells and reduces energy output — meaning higher power bills and a longer payback period on your system. Our professional cleaning restores clarity to the panel surface and helps your system operate at peak efficiency.

We use only manufacturer-approved, non-abrasive methods, so your panels and warranty remain fully protected throughout the process.

How It Works

Our Solar Panel Cleaning Process

A careful, professional process designed to maximise output without risking your panels or warranty.

01
Safe Roof Access
We establish safe access and work positioning on your roof, following all relevant safety procedures before touching a panel.
02
Gentle Pre-Rinse
A gentle pre-rinse with purified water loosens surface dust, debris, and bird droppings without abrasion or high pressure.
03
Soft Wash & Wipe
Non-abrasive, panel-safe equipment lifts stubborn grime and salt residue without scratching glass or damaging cells.
04
Purified Rinse & Inspect
A final purified water rinse leaves a streak-free finish. We inspect every panel and report any damage or concerns found.

Coastal Properties

Peninsula Properties Need
More Frequent Care

Due to the Peninsula's coastal climate, properties near the water are more heavily exposed to salt spray. Salt residue creates a film on solar panels that reduces efficiency and, over time, may contribute to surface wear if left untreated.

We assess your property and provide a tailored maintenance recommendation based on your location, exposure level, and system size.

Standard Residential
Once every 12 months
Suitable for most inland Peninsula properties with moderate tree and bird exposure.
Coastal Locations
Every 6–9 months
Recommended for properties near the water — areas like Rye, Mount Martha, and Safety Beach where salt spray is heavier.
Commercial & Heavy Exposure
More frequently as needed
Larger systems or properties with heavy tree coverage may benefit from more regular visits to maintain peak output.
