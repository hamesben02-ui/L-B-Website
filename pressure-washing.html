Soft Washing

The Right Pressure for Every Surface

Not every surface can withstand high-pressure washing. For more delicate materials, we use soft washing — a lower-pressure technique paired with professional cleaning solutions to safely remove dirt, mould, and staining without risk of damage.

  • Roof tiles & terracotta
  • Rendered or painted surfaces
  • Timber decking & cladding
  • Lightweight or older paving materials

Surface Safe

Low pressure protects paint, render, and fragile materials from cracking, pitting, or stripping.

Chemical Treatment

Professional-grade solutions break down biofilm, mould, and lichen at the root — not just on the surface.

Longer-Lasting Results

Treating the source means growth takes significantly longer to return compared to pressure-only methods.

Safety First

Removing Mould & Slippery Surfaces

Mould, algae, and lichen don't just look bad — they create genuinely dangerous slip hazards on driveways, pool surrounds, paths, and steps. On the Mornington Peninsula, the coastal climate means these issues are common and return quickly without proper treatment.

We use a combination of pressure washing and targeted biocide treatments to eliminate mould and algae at the root. This approach doesn't just clean the surface — it kills the spores that cause regrowth, keeping your surfaces safer for longer.

  • Pool surrounds & decking (high slip-risk areas)
  • Shaded driveways & paths
  • Steps & entry areas
  • Retaining walls & garden edges

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Will pressure washing damage my pavers or concrete? +
Not with a professional operator. We adjust the pressure setting, nozzle angle, and technique based on the surface type. Damage typically occurs when incorrect pressure is used, or the nozzle is held too close. Our team assesses each surface before starting.
How often should I pressure wash my driveway? +
For most residential driveways, once a year is sufficient to keep things looking clean and prevent build-up of oil, algae, and embedded grime. Properties near the coast or surrounded by trees may benefit from more frequent cleaning due to salt spray and organic debris.
Can you remove oil stains from my garage floor? +
Yes. Oil stains require pre-treatment with a degreaser before pressure washing. We apply the appropriate solution, allow it to break down the oil, then wash the surface clean. Results depend on how long the stain has been there and the type of concrete, but we typically achieve significant improvement.
Do you service commercial properties and body corporates? +
Yes. We regularly work with property managers, strata committees, and business owners on scheduled cleaning contracts. We can accommodate early-morning starts and work around trading hours to minimise disruption to your operations.
